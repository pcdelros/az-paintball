Beginner guide

Make first-time paintball feel less confusing.

This page gives new players a simple, practical overview. It should be reviewed against the real field’s rules before being used as official guidance.

Before game day

What new players usually need to know.

Keep the copy simple and direct. Paintball can feel intimidating, so this page should reduce uncertainty and help people arrive prepared.

Clothing

Dress for movement

Recommend long sleeves, flexible pants, closed-toe shoes, and clothes that can get dusty or marked by paint.

Safety

Keep masks on

Explain that masks stay on in active areas and that players should follow staff instructions and field boundaries at all times.

Arizona heat

Plan for hydration

Arizona players should bring water, use shade breaks, and pay attention to heat, especially during warm months.

First-game flow

A calm step-by-step outline works best.

Use this flow to explain what happens from arrival to the first match. Replace with the exact process for the real field.

  1. 1

    Check in and sign waivers

    Players confirm their reservation, complete required forms, and ask staff questions.

  2. 2

    Get rental gear

    Staff assigns marker, mask, air tank, paint, and any required safety equipment.

  3. 3

    Listen to safety briefing

    Players learn mask rules, surrender rules, boundaries, chrono limits, and referee signals.

  4. 4

    Play short rounds

    Most new groups benefit from shorter rounds, simple objectives, and regular water breaks.

Rental checklist

Help visitors understand what is included.

Common rental items

  • Paintball marker
  • Protective mask
  • Compressed air tank
  • Paintballs or paint package

Good items to bring

  • Water bottle
  • Closed-toe shoes
  • Change of clothes
  • Towel or wipes for cleanup
